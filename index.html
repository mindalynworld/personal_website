Hello! My name is Mindy and I am a data science and visualization engineer, a STEAM educator and curriculum developer, and a professional dancer based in the Bay Area, California.

Over the years I have had a wide variety of professional, academic, and volunteer experiences, and recently noticed that they have all fallen into 3 core themes: (1) Data and Technology, (2) Education and Community Building and (3) Arts and Storytelling. These themes have become pillars, anchoring and guiding me through my career path thus far.

I was curious about how my involvement in each of these themes has fluctuated over time. Have I maintained a balance or have certain ones taken up more of my time at different periods of my life? I decided to visualize it with data.

Evolution of My Experience Over Time

How has the proportion of my involvement in each category changed over time?

To create this chart, I grouped the data into quarters (3 month periods). I summed up the weighted scores (some experiences were more heavily focused in one category than another, or required higher/lower time commitment) for each category for all experiences that fell within that period, and divided each category score by the total. This gave me the percentage of time spent on each category each quarter.

Description

This stacked area chart shows how the proportion of time I spent on the three categories: Arts, Technology, and Education through my professional and extracurricular experiences evolved over time (September 2015 to May 2024). This graphic was made using the ggplot2 R library.

Observations

From late 2015 to mid 2019, there is a pretty even balance between the 3 categories. This aligns with my time at Brown, when I held leadership in a variety of education and arts organizations, while pursuing a degree in Computer Science.

From mid 2019 to mid 2022, tech is alloted the most time, reaching a peak in 2022. Education continues to maintain a strong presence through this period, while arts involvement is mostly nonexistent. These are the few years following my graduation, when I worked full time as a software/data engineer. Simultaneously, I worked on side projects with multiple education organizations to develop curriculum/teach, but didn't have a lot of extra time to continue dancing.

From 2023 onwards, arts involvement expands significantly over time while tech shrinks and education stays relatively consistent. This marks my current post-Master's program era where I've rediscovered my passion for dance while continuing work as a STEM educator. Now, I am seeking to once again find balance between these 3 themes in my life.

About This Website

This website is a work in progress. There are many features I'd like to add, design iterations I'd like to try, accesibility standards to meet, and technologies I'd like to integrate.

Design

I started by sketching ideas in a notebook, and using online design tools (Miro, Figma), to create prototypes before actually coding. Much of what I know about design comes from working closely with UI/UX designers in the tech industry, a UI/UX course I took at Brown, and a Data Journalism course on Udacity that I enrolled in earlier this year. But mostly, my ideas come from reading and getting inspired by all of the data viz out on the web! One of my biggest data viz inspirations is Shirley Wu.

Development

I built this website using d3.js. The site is responsive and can be viewed on both mobile and desktop. I am currently working on (1) migrating it to use Svelte, and (2) implementing scrollytelling to guide the narrative flow.

Data

I converted my resume into a structured data set, labeling each experience with the themes it falls into (can be one or multiple) and the time commitment involved. The themes are weighted, so for example, my experience working as a software engineer at education first was 2 parts tech and 1 part education, because that job focused more on my technical skills than education. I used R to process the data and create certain plots, such as the stacked area chart.
