Hello! My name is Mindy, and this is the story of my career journey.

Over the years, I've had a wide variety of experiences across seemingly disparate sectors: I've built web apps for tech companies, written and taught curriculum for nonprofits, conducted urban studies research in South Africa, pursued a master's in spatial data science, and danced professionally in theater productions.

At times, I felt like my experiences were scattered and disconnected, but then I noticed a pattern: all of my work fell within one or more of these 3 core themes: (1) Data and Technology, (2) Education and Community Organizing and (3) Arts and Storytelling.

I was curious about how my involvement in each of these themes has looked like or fluctuated over time. Have I maintained a balance or some taken precedence at different periods of my life? I decided to visualize it with data.

I started by entering all of my experiences into a spreadsheet, tagging them with themes, their level of time commitment, and duration. Then I used it to create the visualizations below.

Evolution of My Experience Over Time

How has the proportion of my involvement in each category changed over time?

Description

To create this chart, I partitioned the data into 3 month periods. Each experience was given a weighted score for each category (some experiences were more focused in one category, or had higher time commitment). I summed the scores for each category for experiences that fell within that quarter and divided by the total to get the % of time spent on each category. Made with the ggplot2.

Category

Observations

Undergraduate (late 2015 - mid 2019): There is an even balance between the categories. This aligns with my time at Brown, when I was pursuing a degree in Computer science while holding leadership positions in a number of education and arts organizations.

Post-Undergraduate (mid 2019 - mid 2022): Tech is the focus, peaking in 2022. Education maintains a strong presence through this period. These are the few years following my graduation, when I worked full time as a software and data engineer and worked on side projects with education organizations to develop curriculum/teach. Arts involvement is mostly nonexistent as I didn't have a lot of extra time to continue dancing.

Post-Master's (2023 - present): Arts involvement begins to expand significantly while tech shrinks and education persists. This marks my current post-Master's program era where I've been dancing at a professional level, while continuing work as a part-time data researcher and STEM educator.

Throughout my life, technology, education, and the arts have served as distinct yet interconnected avenues for driving social impact. Each has shaped my perspective, and I am determined to continue drawing on all three. Many of my experiences lie at the intersection of two of these themes, but rarely do they integrate all three. Data visualization, however, represents the perfect fusion, allowing me to combine my technical expertise with visual design to craft compelling narratives that inform, educate, and inspire.

About This Website

This website is a work in progress. There are many features I'd like to add, design iterations I'd like to try, accesibility standards to meet, and technologies I'd like to integrate.

Design

I started by sketching ideas in a notebook, and using online design tools (Miro, Figma), to create prototypes before actually coding. Much of what I know about design comes from working closely with UI/UX designers in the tech industry, a UI/UX course I took at Brown, and a Data Journalism course on Udacity that I enrolled in earlier this year. But mostly, my ideas come from reading and getting inspired by all of the data viz out on the web! One of my biggest data viz inspirations is Shirley Wu.

Development

I built this website using d3.js. The site is responsive and can be viewed on both mobile and desktop. I am currently working on (1) migrating it to use Svelte, and (2) implementing scrollytelling to guide the narrative flow.

Data

I converted my resume into a structured data set, labeling each experience with the themes it falls into (can be one or multiple) and the time commitment level it involved. The themes are weighted, so for example, my experience working as a software engineer at education first was 2 parts tech and 1 part education, because that job focused more on my technical skills than education. I used R to process the data and create certain plots, such as the stacked area chart.
